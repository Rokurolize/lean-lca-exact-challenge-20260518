# Accepted lessons

- The valid calculus lives on the corrected Verdier class in the bounded homotopy category,
  not on raw chain maps.
- Ordinary finite limits and colimits conflict with the non-split strict extension
  `ℤ ↪ ℝ ↠ 𝕋` once the corrected derived category is triangulated.
- The WPP stability/localization route is not an honest route to ordinary equalizers or
  coequalizers; equalizers of maps between acyclic complexes can be non-acyclic.
- Boundary-topology and localized-adjoint inputs are conditional comparison scaffolding, not
  the intrinsic proof of corrected acyclic triangulated closure.
- The next constructive theorem is mapping-cone/extension closure for
  `exactAcyclicWithCycles`; the Oracle's suggested homotopy-equivalence milestone was already
  present locally and was therefore not adopted as new work.
